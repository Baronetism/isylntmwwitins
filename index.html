your story

Over the years I’ve heard so many beautiful stories of how MCR affected people’s lives, and I’d love to collect and share them. If you want to tell me what this band has been for you, I’d love to share it here on this fan page — under your first name only.

my story

When my parents fought, I’d put my headphones on and turn “Dead!” all the way up, until their voices were muffled under Gerard’s. He could scream louder than either of them — and somehow his was the only screaming that never scared me.

The fights ended, but the hard things didn’t. In the years since, I’ve come through chronic illness, depression, my own battle with addiction, and my mother’s fight with cancer. Through all of it, this album stayed — the same songs at 10 and at 30, still insisting there was a reason to stay. Every time life gave me a black eye, this band iced it.

The Black Parade has been my favorite album since I was ten. Twenty years later I was blessed enough to watch them play it live in Florence, Italy. Some circles take two decades to close; this one was worth every year.

I owe this band more than I know how to repay. They were there in the worst of it, and they showed me the one thing I’ve never let go of: that grief and rage and everything unbearable can be made into art. Into grotesque beauty.

My Chemical Romance taught me that pain isn’t the end of the story, it’s the raw material.

This site’s name — ISYLNTMWWITINS — is the bridge of “Famous Last Words”:

I see you lying next to me, with words I thought I’d never speak.

Fourteen letters for fourteen words — a line that is a whole love letter.
