the story

When my parents fought, I’d put my headphones on and turn “Dead!” all the way up, until their voices were muffled under Gerard’s. He could scream louder than either of them — and somehow his was the only screaming that never scared me.

The Black Parade has been my favorite album since I was ten. Twenty years later I was blessed enough to watch them play it live in Florence, Italy. Some circles take two decades to close; this one was worth every year.

I owe this band more than I know how to repay. They were there in the worst of it, and they taught me the one thing I’ve never let go of: that grief and rage and everything unbearable can be made into art. Into grotesque beauty.

This site’s name — ISYLNTMWWITINS — is the bridge of “Famous Last Words”:

I see you lying next to me, with words I thought I’d never speak.

Fourteen letters for fourteen words — one of the most quietly devastating lines they ever wrote.
